Prompt for Figma Make:

Create a static one-page website for Pythia, an independent art advisory led by Francesca De Filippo. The site serves high-net-worth private collectors and corporations. Tone: quiet luxury, editorial, discreet, high-end minimalism in the spirit of Gagosian, Hauser & Wirth, and Sotheby's private sales pages — not cold or sterile, but composed and confident.

Design system

    Primary color: #0E3931 (deep forest green) as the dominant background or accent
    Text color: white (#FFFFFF) on primary, with
    #0E3931 on white sections if alternating
    Strictly monochromatic — no secondary accent colors
    Typography: Helvetica World (or Helvetica Neue as fallback) throughout. Use weight and scale — not color or decoration — to create hierarchy. Generous letter-spacing on headers, tight leading on body.
    Layout: asymmetric, editorial grid. Wide negative space. Left-aligned text blocks. Thin hairline dividers (1px) only where structurally necessary.
    Imagery: reserve 2–3 placeholder image slots (tall portrait crops, muted/neutral, evoking gallery interiors or abstract art details). Treat images as artifacts, not decoration — framed by whitespace, never full-bleed stacked.
    No shadows, no gradients, no rounded corners beyond 2px, no icons except a minimal hamburger on mobile.

Responsive

    Desktop: 1440px frame
    Mobile: 390px frame
    Ensure both frames are delivered and behave naturally — stacked sections on mobile, preserved hierarchy and whitespace.

Structure — four sections only, in this order:

1. About (hero) Full-viewport opening. Large typographic statement, left-aligned. Use this copy:

    Pythia is an independent art advisory working with private collectors and corporations on acquisitions, sales, commissions, and long-term collection strategy.

Below, in smaller type, a secondary line:

    Pythia is for those who sense that collecting is about more than ownership. If something in you already knows that, this practice is likely for you.

Small "Pythia" wordmark top-left. Minimal anchor links (About · Bio · Approach · Contact) top-right in small caps.

    Francesca trained at the Courtauld Institute in London, where she completed an MA with a dissertation, awarded Distinction, on countercultures in Eastern Europe and Latin America. Her career has moved between the commercial and curatorial edges of the art world — with formative roles at White Cube, Christie's, and Nina Johnson in Miami — alongside a decade building commercial strategy for international businesses across Europe and the Americas.

    She works between Miami, Madrid, and Caracas, and maintains active relationships with artists, galleries, and institutions across Europe, Latin America, and the United States.

Beside or beneath the text, one placeholder portrait image slot (portrait crop, treated as an artwork — generous margins, not cropped tight to the edges).

What to avoid

    No stock-photo feel, no "luxury" clichés (gold, serif italics, marble textures)
    No testimonials, no logos of past employers, no social media icons
    No hero video, no parallax, no animation beyond subtle fade-ins on scroll
    No CTAs with urgency language ("Book now", "Get started") — if a CTA is used anywhere, it should read simply as a link: Write to Francesca →
